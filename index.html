I am packing for eight weeks in Europe, and this trip is going to be full of life. Swimming in azure water off exotic little towns in Croatia. Pintxos, txakoli and a few museum afternoons in Spain. Champagne in Épernay, a bold red in Bordeaux, and lazy days in Antibes on the Riviera. Cathedrals and historic towns in Italy, thermal baths and a slower pace on the island of Ischia. Vineyards, winery tours and gorgeous beaches woven in between. Every place has its own flavour, its own scent, its own pace, and I want a wardrobe that can keep up with all of it.

So instead of pulling together another generic packing list, I decided to build my wardrobe piece by piece with ChatGPT. One item at a time, photographed and reviewed, kept or let go.

It has honestly been one of the more enjoyable projects I have done. I have mixed and matched what I already own, minimised where I could, and left room in my suitcase for a few beautiful things I might find along the way.

So many of you have asked how I am approaching it, so I pulled the whole thing together into one prompt you can use for your own trip. Start a new chat, paste it in, and let it become an ongoing conversation rather than a one time list.

The Philosophy

Pack on purpose, not from fear

Most of us pack from the same worry. What if I need this. So we overpack, we buy things we never touch, and we carry a suitcase full of "just in case." This method works differently. It was never about packing less. It's about packing on purpose, so you end up with one feeling: complete confidence in everything you chose to bring.

Step One

Discover Your Travel DNA

Before a single item gets packed, it helps to understand how you actually travel. The prompt below starts here, your itinerary, your pace, the activities you care about, your comfort and style, how you like to shop while you're away. Everything that follows is built around that.

Step Two

The Passport Method

Eight steps, built to run over several conversations as your wardrobe comes together.

01

Get to know you

Destination, dates, weather, activities, your style, your budget, your luggage. The starting point is you, not a template.

02

Build the wardrobe together

You upload photos of what you already own, one piece at a time. A visual inventory builds gradually rather than all at once.

03

An honest wardrobe editor

Every piece gets a pack, maybe, or leave home, with the reasoning behind it. Duplicates get called out. Nothing gets a pass just to be nice.

04

A true capsule

Your colour palette and gaps become clear. You are encouraged to wear what you already own before anything new gets added to the list.

05

Pack for the person you are

Not just the weather. What you actually reach for, what makes you feel confident, and a gentle challenge if you are packing from habit rather than intention.

06

Everything earns its passport

Does it create outfits, suit the itinerary, and make you excited to wear it? If not, it stays home.

07

Minimise everything, not just clothes

Shoes, makeup, skincare, haircare, electronics. The same thinking applies to the whole suitcase, not just the wardrobe.

08

The final list

A finished packing list, outfit combinations, travel day looks, dinner outfits, and a weather backup plan, ready before you leave.

The Passport Method

  • Does it create more than one outfit?
  • Does it work with several other pieces already packed?
  • Does it suit where you are actually going?
  • Does it earn its place, or is it just a "just in case"?
Step Three

Travel Wellness Capsule

Packing well isn't only about clothes. This is where you pack your health with the same intention, medications and supplements, skincare and haircare, all the small daily things that keep you feeling like yourself while you're away. Decant what you can, choose travel sizes, and only bring what you'll actually use. The same question applies here too. Does it earn its passport?
